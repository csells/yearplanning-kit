# Board Simulation - Year 2026

## Plan Presented
- **Mission:** Create an AI startup with partnership foundation, while maintaining health transformation and work/family boundaries
- **Big Rocks:**
  1. PRO2: "Build with friends, validate with users" - Startup foundation validated
  2. PRO1: "Earn the runway, choose the work" - $200K consulting revenue
  3. PRI1: "Finish strong, be fully present" - 100 lb goal + boundaries working
- **Capacity:** ~237 working days
- **Force Ranking:** PRO2 is #1 ("not even close")

## Round 1: Initial Deliberation

### Board Perspectives

**The Architect:** Identified strategic contradiction—PRO2 ranked first but PRO1 demands 40-60+ billable days (25-40% capacity). Competitive window closing while plan keeps IP proprietary. Partnership structure unclear (5 names, no roles defined).

**The Guardian:** Acknowledged health transformation success. Concerned about PRI1's dual nature—health continuation requires consistency, boundary change requires new patterns. December vacation pattern is structural, not willpower-solvable.

**The Catalyst:** Plan may be too conservative. Agent OS free inference removes major cost constraint. "Validated foundation" feels safe—why not target actual revenue? $200K consulting preserves old identity when plan calls for identity shift.

**The Realist:** Financial pressure is psychological, not actual. Significant retirement savings provide indefinite sustainability. Question: Is $200K real need or padded comfort number?

**The Sage:** Noticed fear embedded in plan. "Discomfort" and "fear" language around savings. Plans built around resolving fear often create what they avoid.

### Chairman Questions
1. What's actual monthly burn rate? Does $200K represent real need or psychological padding?
2. Would $100K consulting feel acceptable, or does the number carry emotional weight?
3. What's the actual mechanism (not intention) for fixing vacation/boundary pattern?

### CEO Response
1-2. Need $200K additional payroll in 2026. Will come from consulting income OR investing in own company via retirement. Prefer consulting.
3. Structure: when it's family vacation time, that's it—not working on non-critical things. Made conscious choice today to be present for April instead of working.

## Round 2: Deeper Probing

### Board Response

**The Realist:** Reframed self-investment: "Investing $200K in your startup" isn't "dipping into savings"—it's capitalizing your company. Offense, not retreat. What's expected return on $200K in Code Army/Eli vs. retirement accounts?

**The Architect:** Consulting trades 50-100 days for cash. Self-investment trades capital for time. At 56 with closing competitive window, time may be worth more than capital.

**The Guardian:** "Things that aren't critical" is a loophole. Who decides what's critical when startup is gaining traction? Also: December pattern wasn't choosing to work—it was boredom leading to laptop. What fills the mental space?

**The Catalyst:** With 50-100 extra days focused on product, could you hit revenue-positive by December? Not just "validated"—actual paying customers?

**The Sage:** "I'd rather do the former" is preference, not necessity. Preferences can be examined. What does investing retirement in your own company mean emotionally?

### Chairman Questions
1. If you invested $200K instead of consulting, what would you do with 50-100 extra days? Be specific.
2. What does investing retirement savings in your company mean emotionally?
3. What's your actual firewall for vacation time—not intention, but structure?

### CEO Response
1. Would get to MVP launch sooner. Not doing consulting for past couple weeks—noticeably more productive without it.
2. "I like that idea. That feels good to me."
3. Experience and judgment—knows difference between what can wait and what can't from previous startups.

## Round 3: Convergence

### Board Response

**The Realist:** "That feels good to me" is significant. Psychological barrier dissolved. $200K invested plus 50-100 days reclaimed. Startup with traction beats 7-10% retirement returns. Reversing earlier caution—financial risk of self-investment is lower than opportunity cost of consulting.

**The Architect:** "Noticeably more productive without consulting" is data, not speculation. PRO1 as written isn't the right form. Suggested reframe: "Fund the runway through self-investment."

**The Catalyst:** "MVP launch sooner" is the answer hoped for. Push: upgrade PRO2 from "validated foundation" to "first paying customers" or "revenue traction by Q4."

**The Guardian:** Trusting experience and judgment on boundaries. Observation: December pattern happened to someone with decades of experience. This work (personal projects, startup dream) may hit differently than past startups.

**The Sage:** "That feels good to me"—the body knows before the mind. Fear of "dipping into savings" was a frame, not a fact. Reframed as "investing in my company," fear dissolves. PRO1 as written may no longer be necessary for its designed purpose.

### Chairman Question
Given what's emerged: Keep PRO1, revise substantially, or remove entirely? If removed, should PRO2 be upgraded?

### CEO Response
Want to revise PRO1—spend time on consulting that's strategically important, e.g., the Jesse work that builds a partnership for potential host of Eli business.

## Round 4: Refinement

### Board Response

**The Architect:** Strategic clarity achieved. Not eliminating consulting—filtering through PRO2 lens. Jesse work isn't competing with Eli; it's building infrastructure for Eli. Consulting becomes tactic within strategy, not separate priority.

**The Catalyst:** "Burn the boats" done intelligently. Filter: "Does this consulting advance Code Army, Eli, or the partnership network?" If yes, consider. If no, decline regardless of money.

**The Realist:** If self-funding $200K and only strategic consulting, what's realistic income from Jesse-type work? The revised PRO1 isn't about revenue number—it's about partnerships with value beyond the invoice.

**The Guardian:** Revision helps PRI1 too. Random consulting is unpredictable. Strategic consulting with partners is controllable—fewer surprise demands on time.

**The Sage:** Given permission to remove PRO1 entirely. Instead, found the piece worth keeping. That's discernment. Jesse example is both—revenue AND partnership, consulting AND startup building. Integration, not compromise.

### Chairman Synthesis
**Original PRO1:** "Earn the runway, choose the work" — $200K consulting
**Revised PRO1:** "Partner through purpose" — Strategic consulting only when it builds startup infrastructure/partnerships. Self-fund runway. Say no to competing consulting.

### CEO Response
"Wisdom is looking at what everyone else is looking at and seeing what nobody else sees." — Neil Degrasse Tyson

To validate, don't need to be in the black, but do need paying customers.

## Round 5: Final Synthesis

### Final Statements

#### The Architect
**Statement:** Plan evolved from tension-filled compromise to coherent strategy. Original PRO1 and PRO2 fought for same resource. Revised structure makes them allies. Not leaving consulting—weaponizing it. Every engagement must pass filter: "Does this build the startup?" Competitive window concern remains; choice to stay proprietary is bet on execution speed.

**Do's:**
- Apply strategic filter ruthlessly to every consulting opportunity
- Define "paying customers" concretely—how many, what price, by when
- Revisit proprietary vs. open source quarterly
- Build in public even if code stays private

**Don'ts:**
- Don't take consulting for money alone
- Don't let "strategic" become loophole
- Don't wait for perfect MVP—paying customers validate, not features
- Don't ignore open source audience-building

Boundary work relies on judgment, not structure. If December pattern repeats, revisit assumption. PRO1 revision helps—predictable strategic consulting easier to schedule around.

**Do's:**

- Schedule vacation Q2 or Q3, not December
- Tell April what "critical" means before vacation starts—shared definition
- Notice if strategic consulting expands to fill available time

**Don'ts:**
- Don't sacrifice sleep or exercise for startup urgency
- Don't assume December 2026 will be different without structural change
- Don't let startup become new excuse for old pattern
- Don't forget: presence is the goal, not physical proximity

#### The Catalyst

**Do's:**

**Don'ts:**

#### The Realist

Risk is real but bounded—not betting the house. Jesse work is partnership infrastructure; price it at relationship worth, not hourly rate.

"That feels good to me"—body knew before mind. Plans built on fear create what they avoid. Plans built on alignment create what they reach toward.
